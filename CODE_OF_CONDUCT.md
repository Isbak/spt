# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in the Semantic Platform Template community a
respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless
of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, sexual
identity and orientation, or technology choices.

We are committed to building a community where people can collaborate safely,
learn from one another, and disagree constructively.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Showing empathy, patience, and respect toward other people.
- Welcoming newcomers and helping them find relevant documentation or prior work.
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback with good intent.
- Focusing criticism on ideas, code, designs, and processes rather than people.
- Acknowledging mistakes, apologizing when appropriate, and learning from the
  experience.
- Respecting project governance, review processes, security reporting channels,
  and maintainer decisions.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption of discussions.
- Demeaning, discriminatory, or insulting comments, jokes, imagery, or gestures.
- Sexualized language or imagery, unwelcome sexual attention, or advances.
- Publishing or threatening to publish another person's private information
  without explicit permission.
- Trolling, personal attacks, or inflammatory comments that derail collaboration.
- Abuse of project infrastructure, review privileges, issue trackers, chat
  channels, or automation.
- Any other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional community setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including repositories,
issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, chat channels, meetings,
conferences, and other events where someone represents the project.

It also applies when public behavior outside project spaces creates a safety
risk for community members or harms the project's ability to collaborate in good
faith.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the maintainers
through the repository owner's preferred private contact channel. If the report
involves a security vulnerability or sensitive security concern, follow the
private reporting process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead.

Reports should include, when safe and available:

- A description of what happened and where it occurred.
- Links, screenshots, logs, or other supporting context.
- The names or handles of people involved.
- Whether the situation is ongoing or time-sensitive.
- Any requested privacy constraints or safety concerns.

Maintainers will handle reports as confidentially as practical and will limit
information sharing to people who need it to review, respond to, or resolve the
matter.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable
behavior. They may take any action they deem appropriate and proportionate,
including:

1. **Correction** — private or public clarification that behavior was
   inappropriate, with a request to stop or adjust it.
2. **Warning** — a formal notice describing the violation and expected future
   conduct.
3. **Temporary restriction** — temporary loss of access to project spaces,
   communication channels, or maintainer/reviewer privileges.
4. **Permanent restriction** — permanent removal from project spaces or
   privileges for repeated, severe, or dangerous conduct.

Maintainers should consider impact, intent, severity, safety risks, and prior
patterns when determining enforcement. Retaliation against someone who reports a
concern or participates in an investigation is itself a violation of this Code
of Conduct.
